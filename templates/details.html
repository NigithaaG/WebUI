PCOS Risk Factors and Preventive Measures

Follicle Number (Right and Left)

The follicle number in the ovaries is an indicator of ovarian health. Normal follicle count is between 5 and 12 on each ovary.

Normal: 5-12 follicles

PCOS Risk: Greater than 12 follicles

Preventive Measures: Regular exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, and a balanced diet can help regulate ovarian function.

Hair Growth

Excessive hair growth, particularly in a male pattern, can be a symptom of PCOS.

Normal: No excessive hair growth

PCOS Risk: Yes (excessive hair growth)

Preventive Measures: Hormonal treatments, maintaining a healthy weight, and reducing insulin resistance can help manage symptoms.

Skin Darkening

Darkened patches of skin can occur with PCOS, often due to insulin resistance.

Normal: No skin darkening

PCOS Risk: Yes (skin darkening)

Preventive Measures: Improving diet, reducing sugar intake, and using topical treatments can help manage this condition.

Weight Gain

Unexplained weight gain is a common symptom of PCOS due to hormonal imbalance and insulin resistance.

Normal: No unexplained weight gain

PCOS Risk: Yes (weight gain)

Preventive Measures: Regular physical activity, a balanced diet low in processed foods and sugars, and monitoring insulin levels can help manage weight.

Menstrual Cycle Regularity

Regular menstrual cycles are a sign of healthy reproductive function. Irregular cycles can be a symptom of PCOS.

Normal: Regular cycles

PCOS Risk: Irregular cycles

Preventive Measures: Hormonal treatments, maintaining a healthy weight, and reducing stress can help regulate menstrual cycles.

Luteinizing Hormone (LH) Levels

LH is a hormone that helps regulate the menstrual cycle and ovulation.

Normal: Less than 10 mIU/ml

PCOS Risk: Greater than 10 mIU/ml

Preventive Measures: Balanced diet, regular exercise, and possibly medication to regulate hormone levels.

Fast Food Consumption

Frequent consumption of fast food is linked to higher risk of obesity and insulin resistance, which can contribute to PCOS.

Normal: No or limited fast food consumption

PCOS Risk: Frequent fast food consumption

Preventive Measures: Healthy eating habits, focusing on whole foods and reducing processed food intake.

FSH to LH Ratio

The ratio of Follicle Stimulating Hormone (FSH) to LH is an important indicator in diagnosing PCOS.

Normal: Around 1:1

PCOS Risk: Greater than 1:2

Preventive Measures: Lifestyle changes, healthy diet, and possible medical intervention to regulate hormones.

Cycle Length

The length of menstrual cycles can indicate reproductive health. Normal cycles range from 5 to 7 days.

Normal: 5-7 days

PCOS Risk: Greater than 10 days or less than 3 days

Preventive Measures: Regular physical activity, balanced diet, and stress management can help maintain regular cycle length.
